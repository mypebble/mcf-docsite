Joinos - Help for Donors

Tip: You can view screens on this page at full size by clicking on the thumbnail image.

Donating through the Joinos Web Page
Altruism is a means of feeling good about yourself and at the same time knowing you are helping to make a difference to a worthy cause. In addition, the UK government rewards the altruism of donation by providing relief on the tax paid on donations to charities from UK taxpayers, thereby increasing the revenue to the charity by 25%. This costs ordinary rate taxpayers nothing. Higher rate taxpayers receive tax refunds of the difference between the rate they pay and the basic rate on the donation. Gift Aid is a win-win situation.

The Joinos Web Page is a web page where you can donate to a school or academy project easily.

Access the Joinos Web Page

You can access this web page directly from the school or academy website, or if you know the URLAn address for an item on the Internet. you can type this into the browser directly.

Joinos - Web Page Elements

The Web Page has a number of elements which help you identify the project you want to donate to.

Header

The header contains the project title and a summary extracted from the project narrative. In addition, the first donor button is listed at the right hand side so you don't have to scroll down in order to make a donation. In the header you can also see the current amount that has been donated so far along with the number of donors and the number of days the project is still open to donations.

Narrative

The narrative section of the web page contains two tabs of information. The first tells the story behind the project and the second lists previous donors and their supportive comments for the project.

Footer

The footer contains links to web pages you may find useful, such as links to past projects. There are contact email addresses if you have a query about the website. There are also links to social media so you can easily share information about the project and your donation to it.

Donation buttons

The first donation button is in the header, so you don't need to scroll down the web page in order to make a donation. The second donation button is just above the footer. Clicking on either opens the Donate screen.

Joinos - Make a Donation

  1. Click Donate. The Donation screen opens.

  2. Enter the Amount you want to donate.

    3. The amount defaults to £10 but you can change this to any amount you want to donate. You can either enter an amount in the Amount box or use the mouse to select the slider and increase or decrease the amount you want to donate.

  3. Enter your First Name and Last Name.
  4. If you want to donate anonymously, select the check box Anonymous Donation.

    5. This means that your name won't appear on the list of donors on the Donors tab but will appear alongside the text Anonymous Donation.

  5. Enter your Email Address.

    6. This is needed so that your donation receipt can be sent to you.

  6. If you are a UK taxpayer, you can boost your donation by 25% by allowing the school or academy to reclaim 25% of the donation amount from HMRC. To do this, select the checkbox, I am a UK Tax Payer.
    • If you selected the checkbox, enter your Postcode in the box that appears and click Find Address.
    • Select your address from the Postcode Results.

    Note: By selecting I am a UK Tax Payer and entering your address you are making a legal declaration of your tax paying status.

  7. Click Make Donation.

    8. The Donation Details and Card Details screens appear.

    1. Enter your card details - Card Number, Name on Card, Expiry Month and the CVV security number on the back of the card.
    2. Select I agree to the terms and conditions of this payment in order to make payment.

    Note: To read the terms and conditions, click Read Terms.

  8. Click Make Payment.

    9. If payment is successful you will see a Thank you screen like this.

    • To leave a message with your donation, enter the text in the Leave a Comment box and click Submit. Your message will be visible on the Donors tab in the Narrative on the web page.
    • To share your donation on Twitter, click Twitter.
    • To share your donation on Facebook, click Facebook.
