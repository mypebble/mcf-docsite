The Importance of Relationships with Contacts
Income may be received through your relationships. In order to maximise the engagement and contributions from your contacts, you need to understand and profile the different types of relationships you have with them. You will have contacts to whom you are accountable. In other words you will be expected to contribute towards these bodies’ aims. You will have other contacts with whom you conduct transactions i.e. you will provide goods or services and in return they will give you cash, equipment, resources, facilities or materials. Finally you will have contacts who are looking for a deeper level of engagement and participation in your school than simply giving you money. These are known as developmental contacts who will want to be involved in the planning and delivery of your projects and activities.

The Contacts module is where you manage all your contact details, including your contact's marketing preferences.

Note: It is your responsibility to handle personal data in accordance with GDPR law.

Create a contact

      • If you don't know if the contact is eligible, leave blank for now as you can find out and fill this in later.
      • If you have a Gift Aid declaration for the contact in PDF format, you will be able to upload this once you have created the contact details.

      See Managing Gift Aid Declarations.

Managing Gift Aid Declarations

Gift Aid is a tax concession where the UK government will not collect 25% in tax on donations where the donor is a UK taxpayer and the recipient has charitable status.

What are the legal requirements?

  1. Check your organisation is eligible to receive Gift Aid donations.
    2. Are you eligible to receive gift aid on donations?

    The HMRC has guidance on the www.gov.uk website on all the rules around claiming Gift Aid. In summary, to claim Gift Aid:

    • You must be recognised as a charity or community amateur sports club (CASC) for tax purposes.

      • Academies get a charity number assigned when they set themselves up as an academy. Maintained schoolsA school in England or Wales that is funded by a local education authority. cannot become charities but may be able to acquire a form of charitable status through an associated Friends of trust. For more information, click here.

  2. Collect declaration documents that record who is giving you donations which are eligible for Gift Aid.
    • If you collect a Gift Aid declaration document from a donor and make a Gift Aid claim, the donor is responsible for the statement that they are a UK taxpayer within the given tax year of making the donation. If the statement isn’t true, HMRC will ask the donor to pay the equivalent amount.
      • Gift Aid only equates to 25% of the amount donated. A donor would only be liable to pay the Gift Aid amount if they paid less tax in the current year than the amount of Gift Aid claimed on all their donations in the same year. For example, if a donor made five donations to the same charity in the between 5 April 2017 and 6 April 2018 at £12 each time and claimed eligibility for Gift Aid, you would receive £15 each time (£12 from the donor and £3 from HMRC) or £75 (£60 from the donor and £15 from HMRC). If the donor pays less than £15 tax in the tax year 5 April 2017 to 6 April 2018, he or she would be eligible to pay only the £15 given as Gift Aid instead of HMRC providing it.
    • If you collect Gift Aid without collecting a declaration from the donor, you are responsible and HMRC may ask you to repay the equivalent Gift Aid amount.
    Tip:

    You can collect Gift Aid declaration documents back-dated up to four years previously and claim Gift Aid on eligible donations made during that time. Tax years in the UK run from 6 April in one year to 5 April in the next year. So if you are now in May 2018, you can collect Gift Aid on donations made since 6 April 2015(6 April 2015 - 5 April 2016, 6 April 2016 - 5 April 2017, 6 April 2017- 5 April 2018, and any donations since 6 April 2018.

    Note:

    You need to ensure that you have the correct registration for the donations made up to and since April 2016.

    In April 2016, text was changed in Gift Aid declarations to be more robust and include wording to confirm that you are a tax payer and understand that if you pay less tax in a year than the amount of Gift Aid claimed for your donation, you are liable to pay the Gift Aid claimed. For example, the declaration text would include something like this.:

    I am a UK taxpayer and understand that if I pay less Income Tax and/or Capital Gains Tax than the amount of Gift Aid claimed on all my donations in that tax year it is my responsibility to pay the difference.

    If you obtained a Gift Aid declaration from a donor before April 2016, this declaration is valid for the donations made by the donor up to April 2016. You should obtain a new declaration for any donations made by the donor after April 2016.

  3. Keep declaration documents for six years from the end of the accounting period they relate to.

    4. If you have a declaration that covers a repeating donation, you must keep these always. If the donor stops donating, then you must keep their declaration for six years following the end of the accounting period in which they made their last donation.

    You must ensure that you are able to access all Gift Aid declarations for the accounting period in which a donation is made, and evidence the details when required for auditing purposes.

Collect Gift Aid declaration documents

You can do this in a number of ways:

Issue hard-copy declarations to donors to fill in

  1. Select an existing contact in the contacts list.
  2. Click Send gift aid declaration alongside the Gift Aid check box.
  3. Click Download A Gift Aid Declaration template.
  4. Print this document and issue to donors for them to complete.

  5. When you receive the hard-copy declaration filled in, scan it and then upload it.
    • From the Send gift aid declaration option on the contact details screen, attach the declaration to the contact by clicking Browse and selecting the scanned file.

Send the online declaration form to individual donors to fill in

  1. Select an existing contact in the contacts list.
  2. Click Send gift aid declaration alongside the Gift Aid check box.
  3. The email details re pre-filled for you. If you need to change anything, you can.

  4. Click Send.

Use the Gift Aid email campaign to collect Gift Aid declarations from groups of Contacts

For more information, see Email Campaigns.

Note: Since 25 May 2018 you must obtain marketing consent from your contacts before sending them any communications in this way. If you haven't already obtained this, you are prompted in Arro to run the Marketing Consent campaign before you run the Gift Aid campaign.

Collect online confirmation along with online donations

When someone donates to a project by clicking on the Donate button on the project Community Page, they are asked in the online form to confirm that they are eligible to give Gift Aid.

Marking contacts as active or inactive (archived)

By default when you create a contact, the contact is active (in use). You can mark a contact as archived and then active again. This means that you can create and hold a full list of contacts even if people are occasionally not active.

Mark active contacts as inactive (archived)

  1. Open Supporters > Contacts.

  2. On the Active tab, select the contact name to mark inactive.

    3. Tip: Filter the list of names displayed by entering the name or characters from the name into the Search box.

    Note: You can select multiple names.

  3. Select Mark Inactive. The contact names move from the Active to the Archived tab.

Mark archived contacts as active

  1. Open Supporters > Contacts.
  2. Select the Archived tab.
  3. Select the names of the contacts you want to mark as active.
  4. Select Mark active.

Import Data

If you have a lot of contacts to set up you can do this in one go by importing the information from a CSV (comma separated values) file provided you set it up correctly.

You can create a Microsoft Excel spreadsheet with the extension .csv to hold the information you want to import. The first line in the file must be the header which specifies the fields you intend to import. Then each contact’s information must be entered on a new line in the csv file.

If you want to create a file of your own, you can do this by downloading the template from Contacts > + Add contact > Import > Download template.

Records are assigned unique identifier numbers (IDs) in the import file. All records with unique IDs are appended to the existing list of contacts within Contacts. If the ID in the import file is not unique and matches one already in Contacts, then the existing record is overwritten. You can see the unique ID of existing records by exporting the contact list.

You cannot import marketing preferences for contacts and must add these manually once contact information has been imported. You should only update this information if you have received a direct request from the contact to do so.

Note: It is your responsibility to handle personal data in accordance with GDPR law.

Creating your own import file

  1. OpenSupporters > Contacts> + Add contact > Import.
  2. Click Download Template

    3. A contact template .xslx is downloaded to your PC.

  3. Open this template .xslx and save it to your PC with a name of your choosing and the Save as type CSV (comma delimited).
  4. Enter the details of the contacts you want to import.

    5. Remember the information is entered for each name on a separate line.

    e.g.

    Header

    Contact 1 information

    ….

    Contact N information

Importing data

  1. Open Supporters > Contacts > + Add contact > Import.
  2. If you have pending imports - imports that you have uploaded already but not imported yet - click View Pending and continue from step 6.
  3. Otherwise, click Browse to choose a file … and browse to select the .csv file you want to import.
  4. Select the file and click Open.
  5. Click Upload.

    6. You will see a message saying the file has uploaded a number of records successfully.

  6. A list of contacts to be imported is then displayed across two tabs: New and Updated.
    1. Select the tab.
    2. To remove records:
      • To remove all records from the import, click Remove All.
      • To remove selected records from the import, select the records you want to remove and click Remove Selected.
    3. To import records:
      • To import all records, click Import All.
      • To import selected records, select the records you ant to import and click Import Selected.

    Note: Records remain pending until they have all been imported or removed from both New Contacts and Updated Contacts.
