Glossary

    A
  • Alumni
    The term used to describe former pupils at a school.
  • Arro
    Pebble's activity management software, helping you to generate income for your projects. Arro has a number of modules: Clubs, Donations, Events, Grants. Lettings, Projects, Services and SRM.
    • B
  • Bids
    Offers at a particular price for something, normally at auction or in a tender.
  • Box
    Another name for an entry field in software e.g. a text box where you can enter text information to be used by the program.
  • Browser
    A program, sometimes known as a web browser, which lets you navigate and browse the Internet e.g. Microsoft Internet Explorer, Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox or Apple Safari.
  • Button
    Something which you can click in the program to make something happen e.g. clicck the Save button to save the information.
    • C
  • Campaign
    This describes how you can perform the activities you need in order to reach a goal. Within Pebble's software, campaigns can help you to perform the activities you need in order to raise money. They often produce reports and so are accessible via the reporting options.
  • Charging Structures
    A plan of charges for supply of goods and services.
  • Charity
    An organisation - normally non-profit-making - which has a charitable status within the UK government's definition.
  • Clubs module
    A module within Arro. This module helps you to manage clubs held and run from your school.
  • Comma Separated Value
    The name given to a file which stores values that can be read by computer programs. Each line in the file is a record and each record is separated by a comma.
  • Community Engagement
    The process and the means by which you go about engaging your community in your projects. For example, you might invite local businesses to a meeting to discuss how they might benefit from a particular school project.
  • Contributor
    A person or organisation who contributes to your project.
  • CSV
    The name given to a file which stores values that can be read by computer programs. Each line in the file is a record and each record is separated by a comma.
    • D
  • Dashboard
    A particular screen within a program which provides you with the ability to access and use the features within a module. For example, you can create and manage projects from the Projects dashboard within Arro.
  • Database
    A store of information for use in a computer.
  • Direct Credit
    Payment direct into a person's or organisation's bank account. For example, payment via BACS.
  • Donations module
    A module within Arro. This module helps you to manage financial gifts given to your school.
  • Donor
    A person or organisation who gifts money or time to your school.
    • E
  • ePayment
    Payment made across the Internet, for example, Paypal transactions.
  • Events module
    A module within Arro. This module helps you to manage events that take place at your school.
    • F
  • Favourites
    A collection of things on the computer that you use frequently. Also known as bookmarking Internet web pages. Within Arro, this refers to marking screens as favourites, using a star icon.
  • Field
    The name given to a box where you enter information in a computer program. This may be text, numbers or a date, depending on the type of field.
  • Fiscal Accountability
    Accountability for the way that money in your school is used and managed. This may be the domain of the school business manager but will more likely involve a number of senior management staff within the school.
  • For Profit
    This is used to describe an organisation whose purpose is to make a profit. Charities are not for profit.
  • Form
    This describes a screen of information that appears within a computer program. Called a form because normally it requires you to enter some information as you do with a paper form.
  • Fund Manager
    Pebble's fund management software, helping you to manage the income that comes in and goes out of your school.
    • G
  • Gift Aid
    This is a UK government scheme which lets registered charities reclaim a percentage of tax on donations from UK taxpayers.
  • Grant
    A grants is a specific amount of funding given to an organisation for specific activity to be delivered within a specific time frame. Funders are usually government departments, local authorities, other large charities or charitable trusts.
  • Grant Writing
    The process of writing an application for a grant.
  • Grants module
    A module within Arro. This module helps you to find and manage applicable grants for your projects.
    • H
  • Handling Time
    The amount of time it takes to perform an action within the process of managing your project activities or grant or sponsorship applications.
  • HMRC
    Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs department within the UK government.
    • I
  • Icon
    A button in a computer program which represents an action or an object and is usually identified by a picture.
  • In-App
    Something which occurs inside a computer program, normally inside a mobile application.
  • Income Generation
    A term used to describe activities you undertake to raise money for your school projects.
  • Income Generation Budgets
    The amounts of money you want to raise through the particular income generating activities within your projects.
  • Income Patterns
    Recognisable trends within income e.g. seasonal income opportunities.
  • Intercom
    Pebble's online support platform. This is what you use when you communicate with our support team.
  • Interface
    This is what you see when you open a program. It is what you use to communicate with the computer.
    • J
  • Joinos
    Pebble's online payment software, helping you to manage the income you receive from parents.Using Joinos you can set up a Web Page with a Donations button, helping you to collect donations and advertise clubs, events, services and lettings.
    • L
  • Lettings
    Hiring out your schools facilities and equipment.
  • Lettings module
    A module within Arro. This module helps you to manage lettings of your premises or resources to outside organisations.
    • M
  • Messaging
    A form of communicating. This may be messages received by email, text or within an application when something needs to be communicated to you.
  • MISapp
    An integration program which allows you to use your school databases within Pebble Fund Manager or Pebble Arro.
    • N
  • Negative Payments
    Payments where monies are returned to the pupil for overpayment or refund.
    • O
  • Offline
    An activity which takes place on a computer while the computer is not connected to the Internet.
  • Online Payment
    Another term for ePayment.
    • P
  • Panel
    A part oof the program window or screen.
  • ParentPay
    An ePayment system which allows parents (also staff) to pay for items online.
  • Platform
    In software this is the operating system on which the computer programs run e.g. Microsoft Windows.
  • Pop-up
    A window in the computer program which pops up on top of another window on a computer screen.
  • Project Narrative
    The elevator pitch of your project. This is the succinct piece of text which encapsulates the reasons you have the project, what needs it meets and what impact it will have.
  • Projects module
    A module within Arro. This module helps you to create your project narrative, and manage all the information about your project from one place.
  • Prompt
    A pop-up window or message that you see in the computer program that prompts you to do something e.g. confirm that you want to delete something.
    • R
  • Relationship Managment
    Management of your customers' or stakeholders' details e.g. hold a databse of their contact details.
  • Response Time
    The amount of time that it takes to receive a response to an action within the process of managing your project activities, grant or sponsorship applications. In software, the amount of time it takes for the program to respond to your action request.
    • S
  • Screen
    A window of the computer program e.g. the dashboard of a module.
  • Services
    Hiring out your school's resources e.g. catering.
  • Services module
    A module within Arro. This module helps you to manage all the services your school provides.
  • SIMS.net
    A school information management system, developed by Capita.
  • Social Media
    A number of applications that exist for online communication and sharing of information e.g. Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn.
  • Sponsorship
    Giving or being in receipt of financial support.
  • sQuid
    An ePayment system which allows parents to pay for items using an online portal.
  • SRM module
    The Stakeholder Relationship Management module within Arro. This module helps you to manage all the details about your stakeholders e.g. contact details and whether they qualify for gift aid. See also Stakeholder and Gift Aid.
  • Stakeholder
    A person or organisation who has an interest in your projects.
  • Star
    An icon within Arro screens which you can use to bookmark or mark the screen or item as a favourite. You can then view those items or screens by choosing to view starred items.
  • Subscription
    A regular financial payment in advance for goods or services.
  • Support
    Material assistance for your projects. Or in the case of software queries, advice and guidance on how to provide a solution.
    • T
  • Tab
    In software, this is the term given to sections of information that can be opened on one screen, or in one browser.
    • U
  • Uniform Resource Locator
    An address for an item on the Internet.
  • URL
    An address for an item on the Internet.
  • User Interface
    This is what you see when you open a program. It is what you use to communicate with the computer.
  • User Permissions
    Allowance that is given to users to perform specific actions within a software program. This is usually given by a system administrator user and certain actions may only be performed by administrator users.
    • V
  • Volunteer
    A person or organisation willing to help you achieve your project results without being paid.
    • W
  • Web Browser
    A program, sometimes known as a web browser. which lets you navigate and browse the Internet e.g. Internet Explorer, Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox or Apple Safari.
  • White Papers
    These are reports which give information and offer guidance for proposals.
  • Window
    A screen in the computer program e.g. the dashboard of a module.
