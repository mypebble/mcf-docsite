Managing Accounts

All the functions for setting up Accounts in Fund Manager are on the Administration Tab and the details of some accounts may have been added during your Set Up. Other Accounts can be added as and when required.

There are three sections, Manage Account, Manage Bank Accounts and Manage Ledger Codes.

An Account is equivalent to an individual column header in a manual ledger. Accounts can be created at any time. There is no effective limit to the number of Accounts you may have, but each one must have its own unique Code.

The Account Code is a combination of between 1 and 6 letters and numbers. Once saved, a code and an account name can be edited but can never be deleted; Instead Accounts are taken ‘Out of Use’ when you have no further need of them. This prevents them from appearing on the transaction screens. They may continue to appear on the report screens if they have a balance which is relevant to the open financial period.

A Bank Account can be any account held with a bank that is used for the purposes of managing your Fund, eg current account, Savings accounts etc.

The Ledger Codes option is available for providing exports into other finance packages to allow you to continue providing unified reports (eg VAT reporting), whilst gaining the benefits of Fund Manager's superior transaction entry. Set up your ledger codes in Fund Manager to match the Ledger codes in your external finance package. Then you can assign debit and credit entries to the correct ledgers to make accounting easier (e.g. grouping all trip transactions etc).

To view and edit your Accounts;

On the Dashboard (Organisation Summary) > Accounts

OR

Go to Administration > Manage Accounts

Here you will see all the Accounts that have been set up in your Fund Manager.

By clicking on any of the Account Names you can then edit the Account details, mark it ‘Out of Use’, Share Balances (with Joinos), Create and Assign to a Group, Assign or change an Opening Balance, Budget and Unit Cost. The Unit Cost needs to be recorded if you would like to use the ‘Who’s Not Paid’ Report in the reporting section (see section on Reporting).

Opening Balance

You can view or amend your Opening Balance here. This is the amount of money that account owned or owed on the date on which the account was created.

 Budget value

You may add details of the Budget value - a negative Budget value indicates an expected level of expenditure or loss. A positive Budget value indicates an expected level of income or profit. This might be useful for budget holders to know their budget position by simply looking in Fund Manager for the funds that remain available.

 Unit Cost

You may add a Unit Cost if appropriate for a trip or activity Account. You can enter the cost per head of this trip or activity. This information will be included in the Who’s Not Paid Report and used to calculate an outstanding balance. If you do not use a unit cost then the Who’s Not Paid Report in the Report tab will not generate any details for you to view.

Click Save or Abandon to go back to the Manage Accounts screen.

Group Actions

You can create a Group for your Accounts, e.g. Trips, off site, overseas, 6th form etc.

Groups are then used with the filter options throughout the application to quickly create reports. The Accounts in the Group will be reported individually and with a subtotal.

Create your Group first and then click the Accounts you want to be added to that Group. Accounts can be quickly added or removed from groups. Once an Account has been added to a group you will see the details in the group’s column beside the account names. You can have multiple groups against each name.

When you see the accounts list throughout the application, it will be presented in account code order. Numbers then letters, alphabetically.

If you need a new Group you can create one in the same way as you can Manage Names (see Manage Names section), from the bottom of the Manage Accounts screen.

Period End Closedown

You will need to close down your Fund Manager at some point after your Period End.

Once the end date for your financial period has passed you can perform a Period End Closedown in Fund Manager that will draw a line under your transactions and allow you to produce the necessary reports to submit to your auditor.

The objective of a closedown is to:

  1. Draw a line under your transactions for the period
  2. Close any accounts that will not be used in the next period
  3. Determine which accounts will have their balances transferred to the new period
  4. Balance your income and expenditure for the period with that of your bank

During the Period End Closedown, Fund Manager will automatically carry forward your account closing balances and present them as the opening balances in the next period

Once you select 'Confirm' your system is locked down into a draft close down, this is not fully closed and may be undone at any time. However, your transactions are moved into an archive state. Once your auditors have fully agreed and signed off your paper work, you may then close down your system completely.

You are still able to enter transaction in your current financial year while this close down is occurring.

Once you have confirmed your Period End Closedown all transactions within the closed period date range are completely locked and you will be no longer able to amend any transactions in the closed period or roll back to an unclosed date.

Before you start to do your Closedown, there is some preparation to do:

  1. Determine which accounts are to be closed
  2. Clear any closing balances which are not required in the next financial period
  3. Determine which accounts are to be retained with their balance in the next financial period
  4. Reconcile all bank accounts up to or beyond the close down date
  5. Print out the list of Cancelled Transactions if necessary

1 - Determine which accounts are to be closed

Go to Reports > Search Account Balances

Put the Period Dates into the date fields (you can see your period beginning date on the Dashboard in the Transactions section).

You can verify here if the closing balances are correct for the start of your new financial period. Tick the Include Out of Use box and click Search

The ‘Out of Use’ accounts appear greyed out on this report.

Now you can see all the Accounts each with their closing Balance (column on the extreme right).

You can verify here if:

  • the closing balances are correct for the start of your new financial period
  • the account will be used in the next financial period

For those accounts that are not to be used in the next period AND have a balance of zero you just need to confirm they have been marked ‘Out of Use’, (greyed out on this report).

To take an account Out of Use:

  1. Go to Administration > Manage Accounts
  2. Click on the box beside the account(s) that you want to take Out of Use (note you can see the account balances here)
  3. Click in the Radio button beside ‘Out of Use’
  4. Click Update Account(s) button (note the accounts will become greyed out)

If you want an Account balance to be something other than the closing balance on the report you will need to make an Account Transfer.

To clear a balance to zero, transfer money out of the Account, to increase the balance transfer money in. You can create a temporary holding Account for these Transfers to go into until you have determined the appropriate accounts to be used;

  1. Go to Administration > Manage Accounts > Create New Account
  2. Create the New Account with a Code e.g. TEMP – End of Year Temporary Account then Save

Please record all these Account Transfers with a date within the financial period you are closing.

2. Clear any closing balances which are not required in the next financial period

To Transfer money between Accounts;

  • Go to Transactions > Transfer Money Between Accounts

If you need to Transfer any Balances please record all these Account Transfers with a date within the financial period you are closing

3. Determine which accounts are to be retained with their balance in the next financial period

Return to Reports > Search Account Balances and make sure the box for ‘Include Out of Use’ is ticked, otherwise you may not see all of your accounts. The ‘Out of Use’ accounts appear greyed out on this report.

Put the Period Dates into the date fields, (you can see your period beginning date on the Dashboard).

You should check that all the accounts are listed with their balance that is to be retained in the new financial period and appear greyed out if they are no longer required.

4. Reconcile all bank accounts up to or beyond the close down date

All your bank accounts must be reconciled to or past your Period End Date.

If you have a Savings Account or an Inactive account where the balance hasn’t changed since you have last reconciled it you must reconcile the bank account with an end date either on or after your close down date.

You can also do a bank reconciliation without any transactions. Please only do this if there has been no change in the account balance, otherwise please request a statement from you bank or wait until you receive your statement.

To do this select the same amount for the closing balance as the opening balance.

  • Go to Bank & Cash > Reconcile a Bank Statement
    • Select a Bank Account from the drop down list
    • Click Reconcile This Account
    • Enter the appropriate statement details with an end date ON or AFTER the close down date
    • Complete the reconciliation as usual by selecting the items which appear on the statement (or if reconciling a zero item statement then simply press Reconcile).

You must do this for ALL your bank accounts. You must complete one reconciliation before you can start another irrespective of how many bank accounts you have. When this is complete you can proceed to create a Draft Period End report.

5. Print out the list of Cancelled Transactions if necessary

If you need a list of all the transactions that you have cancelled throughout the year you will need to print this before closing the period or beginning the Period End closure routine. To do this go to Reports > List of Cancellations.

How to Create a Draft Period End Closedown

You will need to create a Draft Period End Closedown which will generate all the Reports required for a financial period.

This includes :-

  • Accounts Summary Income and Expenditure Report
  • Transactions Report
  • Account Balances
  • Bank History
  • Cash History
  • Closing Bank Balances

These reports will be available at all times on the Period End Closedown menu option.

Once you have created the draft closedown the system locks all the transactions within that time period until you either confirm the closedown after it has been approved, or cancel the draft period end closedown.

To proceed to create a draft period end closedown,

  1. Go to Administration > Period End Closedown
  2. Select Period End Date (Note the Period Beginning Date is populated automatically)
  3. Click on Confirm Button

This page will show you your Account Summary in six sections;

Section 1) - this is your Income and Expenditure Report

Section 2) - this is your Bank and Cash Report with their b/f (brought forward) and c/f (carried forward) Balances

Section 3) - this is your Unreconciled items Report, these are the Transactions which have been recorded in the period about to be closed, but which have not yet appeared on a Bank Statement

‘Amount owed by the Fund’ These are the cheques you have written but that have not yet been presented to the bank ‘Amount owing to the fund’ These are the monies you have received but not yet added to a paying in slip and banked.

The Current Account Balance in Section 1 is the balance as at the end of the period and includes all unreconciled items.

In the above example, the total balance of £48,477.55 is made up of:

  • £783.35 Cash in hand
  • £1,414.80 Unsettled ePayments,
  • Current Account balance of £14,278..40 (with a last reconciled balance of £14,117.60 plus unreconciled credits of £160.80)
  • Budget account balance of £32,001

To see your unreconciled items for the period go to Bank & Cash > Bank History

  1. Select the bank account
  2. Select Unreconciled from the options in the Status box
  3. Select your period start and end dates in the Date From and Date To boxes

Section 4, 5 and 6) these are the sections that should be Signed and Confirmed by the appropriate people, you can print these pages from the bottom of the screen.

You have now successfully created a Draft Period End Closedown and you may no longer edit any transactions dated within your Closedown Dates unless you cancel your draft period.

Click on See All Reports at the bottom of the page to view the full set of Period End Reports

Transactions Report

The transaction report shows the volume and value of transactions that have occurred within the period.

 Account Balances

The Account Balances Report shows the opening balances, receipts and payments, movement in period and closing balances for all accounts, remember to tick to show Include Out of Use and Include Transfers as necessary and click Search

The Bank History Report shows the debits and credits associated to each bank account within the period.

We would strongly recommend you await audit approval before you confirm your Period End Closedown

Completing your Closedown

Once you have had audit approval and are ready to proceed with the Closedown of this financial period;

  1. Go to Administration > Period End Closedown
  2. Click on the Account Summary Report
  3. Click on the Close Down Button at the bottom of the page

You will now see a prompt notifying you that;

When you Closedown a period, you can no longer record any transactions within the stated date range. Note this action cannot be reversed.

If you are you sure you want to continue;

Click on the Close Down Button to Confirm

You will now see under the Archive Section an asterisk in the far right hand column labelled Closed which confirms you have successfully Closed Down this period in Fund Manager.

All the reports will remain on this screen, with the most recently closed period appearing at the top. You can print and re-print these reports as required.

The Who’s not Paid report is NOT affected by closedown dates and will continue to report transactions spanning a number of periods.

ALL other reports will now have a default date range that begins on the new period start date.
