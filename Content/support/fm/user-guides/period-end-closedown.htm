Period End Closedown

Once the end date for your financial period has passed you can perform a Period End Closedown in Fund Manager that will draw a line under your transactions and allow you to produce the necessary reports to submit to your auditor. Once you have confirmed your Period End Closedown, all transactions within the closed period will become locked.

The objective of a closedown is to:

  • Draw a line under your transactions for the period
  • Close any accounts that will not be used in the next period
  • Determine which accounts will have their balances transferred to the new period
  • Balance your income and expenditure for the period with that of your bank

During the Period End Closedown, Fund Manager will automatically carry forward your account closing balances and present them as the opening balances in the next period

Preparing for Period End

Before you can commence with your close down there is some preparation to be done:-

  1. Determine which accounts are to be closed
  2. Clear any closing balances which are not required in the next financial period
  3. Determine which accounts are to be retained with their balance in the next financial period
  4. Reconcile all bank accounts up to or beyond the close down date
  5. Print a list of cancelled transactions

1. Determine which accounts are to be closed

Go to Reports > Search Account Balances and make sure the box for ‘Include Out of Use’ is ticked, otherwise you may not see all of your accounts. The ‘Out of Use’ accounts appear greyed out on this report.

Put the Period Dates into the date fields (you can see your period beginning date on the Dashboard).

Now you can see all the Accounts each with their closing Balance (column on the extreme right).

You can verify here if:-

  • the closing balances are correct for the start of your new financial period
  • the account will be used in the next financial period

For those accounts that are not to be used in the next period AND have a balance of zero you just need to confirm they have been marked ‘Out of Use’, (greyed out on this report).

To take an account ‘Out of Use’

  1. Go to Administration > Manage Accounts
  2. Click on the box beside the account/s that you want to take Out of Use (note you can see the account balances here)
  3. Click in the Radio button beside ‘Out of Use’
  4. Click Update Account/s button (note the accounts will become greyed out)

If you want an account balance to be something other than the Balance on this report you will need to make an account transfer.

In order to clear a balance to zero, transfer money out of the account, to increase the balance transfer money in.

If the account has been marked ‘Out of Use’ you will have to bring it back into use before you can make a transfer.

Not sure which account to use for these transfers? You can create a temporary holding account for these transfers until you have determined the appropriate accounts to be used;

  1. Go to Administration > Manage Accounts
  2. Create New Account – create the new account with a code eg TEMP – End of Year Temporary Account
  3. Save

2. Decide if you need to zero balances or leave them with a true reflection of what has transacted?

You have the opportunity now to decide whether to zero some or all of your account balances or leave these with a true reflection of what has transacted. If you would like to zero account balances you will need to transfer money between accounts.

  1. Go to Transactions > Transfer Money Between Accounts
  2. When transferring any balances please record all these account transfers with a date within the financial period you are closing
  3. If you are not in a position to know which account/s will be used in the transfers of balances, make use of the TEMP account mentioned above or something appropriate to your needs.

3. Determine which accounts are to be retained with their balance in the next financial period

Return to Reports > Search Account Balances and make sure the box for ‘Include Out of Use’ is ticked, otherwise you may not see all of your accounts. The ‘Out of Use’ accounts appear greyed out on this report.

Put the Period Dates into the date fields, (you can see your period beginning date on the Dashboard).

You should check that all the accounts are listed with their balance that is to be retained in the new financial period and appear greyed out if they are no longer required.

4. Reconcile all Bank Accounts up to or beyond the close down date

All your bank accounts must be reconciled to or past your Period End Date.

If you have a Savings Account or an Inactive account where the balance hasn’t changed since you have last reconciled it you must reconcile the bank account with an end date either on or after your close down date.

You can do a bank reconciliation without any transactions. Please only do this if there has been no change in the account balance, otherwise please request a statement from you bank or wait until you receive your statement.

  1. Go to Bank & Cash > Reconcile a Bank Statement
  2. Select a Bank Account from the drop down list
  3. Click Reconcile This Account
  4. Enter the appropriate statement details with an end date ON or AFTER the close down date
  5. Complete the reconciliation as usual by selecting the items which appear on the statement (or if reconciling a zero item statement then simply press Reconcile).

You must do this for ALL your bank accounts. You must complete one reconciliation before you can start another irrespective of how many bank accounts you have. Now you can proceed to create a Draft Period End report.

5. Print a list of cancelled transactions

You will need to print a list of all your transactions that have been cancelled during the year. This must be done before you proceed to Draft Period End Closedown. They will not be available to print after this time.

To do this click Reports > List of Cancellations > Print

Draft Period End

You will need to process a Draft Period End which will generate all the reports required for a financial period. This includes :-

  1. Accounts Summary Income and Expenditure Report
  2. Transactions Report
  3. Account Balances
  4. Bank History
  5. Cash History
  6. Closing Bank Balances

These reports will be available at all times on the Period End Closedown menu option.

Once you have created the draft closedown the system locks all the transactions within that time period until you either confirm the closedown after it has been approved, or cancel the draft period end closedown.

To proceed to create a draft period end closedown go to Administration > Period End Closedown

  • Enter Period End Date (Note the Period Beginning Date is populated automatically)
  • Click on Preview Button Note This is only a preview mode and is NOT the Draft period end report with the signatures.

1. Accounts Summary Income and Expenditure Report

The Account Summary and Expenditure Report includes details of income and expenditure for each account, the balance of all bank accounts including cash / cheques in hand and any unsettled ePayments.

Section 1 Lists all the Accounts in use in this period with their Income and Expenditure

Section 2 Lists your Bank and Cash Accounts with their b/f (brought forward) and c/f (carried forward) Balances

Section 3 Lists the unreconciled bank items, these are the transactions which have been recorded in the period about to be closed, but which have not yet appeared on a bank statement ‘Amount owed by the Fund’ These are the cheques you have written but that have not yet been presented to the bank ‘Amount owing to the fund’ These are the monies you have received but not yet added to a paying in slip and banked.

The Current Account Balance in Section 1 is the balance as at the end of the period and includes all unreconciled items.

In the above example, the total balance of £48,477.55 is made up of:

  • £783.35 Cash in hand
  • £1,414.80 Unsettled ePayments,
  • Current Account balance of £14,278..40 (with a last reconciled balance of £14,117.60 plus unreconciled credits of £160.80)
  • Budget account balance of £32,001

To see your unreconciled items for the period go to Bank & Cash > Bank History

  • Select the bank account
  • Select Unreconciled from the options in the Status box
  • Select your period start and end dates in the Date From and Date To boxes

Placing in a Draft Period End Closedown

If you are satisfied with the information in the Accounts Summary Income and Expenditure Report you can complete your draft Period End Closedown

Click on the Proceed to Closedown Button

This will only create your Draft Period End Closedown it will NOT Closedown your Fund Manager accounts

You will now see 3 additional Sections at the bottom of the Account Summary screen;

Sections 4, 5 and 6, are the sections that should be signed and confirmed by the appropriate individuals responsible for managing and auditing your school funds. You can print this page from the bottom of the screen.

Other buttons at the bottom of the screen are as follows:-

Do not press the Close Down button until your accounts have been audited and duly signed.

If you click on See All Reports at the bottom of the page you will leave this page and return to the screen where the full set of period end reports are listed.

To view and print these reports click on the blue report title link

You can view and print all these reports before and after you finalise your closedown

2. Transactions Report

The transaction report shows the volume and value of transactions that have occurred within the period.

3. Account Balances

The Account Balances Report shows the opening balances, receipts and payments, movement in period and closing balances for all accounts.

4. Bank History

The Bank History Report shows the debits and credits associated to each bank account within the period.

5. Cash History

The Cash History Report shows the debits and credits associated to your Cash In Hand account within the period.

6. Bank Balances

The Bank Balances Report shows the opening and closing balances for the period for each of your bank accounts.

Completing Your Closedown

Once you have had audit approval and are ready to proceed with the Closedown of this financial period go to Administration > Period End Closedown

  • Click on the Account Summary Report
  • Click on the Close Down button at the bottom of the page

You will now see a prompt notifying you that once you close down a period you can no longer record any transactions within the period date range and that a confirmed period end closedown cannot be reversed.

In the Period End menu option you will notice an asterisk in the far right hand column labelled Closed. This confirms you have successfully closed down this period in Fund Manager.

All the reports will remain on this screen, with the most recently closed period appearing at the top. You can print and re-print these reports as required.

The Who’s not Paid report is NOT affected by closedown dates and will continue to report transactions spanning a number of periods.

ALL other reports will now have a default date range that begins on the new period start date.
