Joinos User Guide

Joinos is an e-Payment system which allows Parents/Carers (and even a Staff member) to pay for items using an online portal. This is linked directly to Fund Manager and provides one simple account that links to a child’s school.

Parents can stay up-to-date via computer or mobile phone (using the mobile site). They can make payments online, view their transactions and balances, receive and return permission slips and other documents.

It’s simple, convenient, and it’s completely free for all parents. Joinos benefits everyone. It makes things easier for school and parents/carers but also reduces the administrative burden on the school. helping to cut back on the amount of paper and envelopes sent out.

1 - Registering Joinos Students

Before any parent/carer/ staff member can pay for an offer, they should first be registered on the system.

The student’s Joinos code is then created and given to the Parent/ Carer / Staff member to activate. Once this is done these Students/Staff can then receive offers about items that can be purchased online using Joinos.

There are three tabs which are used to designate the stages of registration. These are Create Joinos Codes, Joinos Codes and Manage Connections.

Create Joinos Codes - This show all the students/staff who are waiting to be registered on Joinos, this includes all new Students/Staff at the School or those who have never been registered.

There is a Show Registered button on this tab and clicking on this will show not only those Students and Staff who have not been registered, but also all who have been registered but have not been activated and those who have an active connection to Joinos due to the Parent/Carer/Staff member logging onto Joinos and activating the Joinos Code.

Joinos Codes - These are all the Students/Staff who have had a Joinos Code created with the Creation and Expiry Date and who have not yet been activated in Joinos.

All codes are valid for 3 months. Any codes not activated within that time will expire and a new code will need to be created as and when necessary.

Manage Connections - these are all the Students/Staff who have been registered and are active within Joinos.

To Register Students/Staff

Click on Administration > Create Joinos Codes and filter for the name required.

If you can't find them in the list click the Show Registered button and they should appear in there.

Click on the Tick box to the left of the name and click Create Joinos Codes button

Their name will now disappear from the Create Joinos Codes tab and a message will appear at the top of the screen to say that “Your Joinos Codes have been created”. You can create more than one code at a time by clicking on the tick box and the names will appear in the white box on the right side of the screen.

Click on the Joinos Codes tab and their name should be showing with the code. An example letter for sending the information for parents should have been given to you during your training. There is also one attached to this User Guide at Appendix A.

Issue: If a parent can’t see their child in Joinos, this is most likely caused by the Joinos code which, if not used, will expire after three months. With regard to the children whose Joinos codes have expired, you will need to recreate their Joinos codes and then inform the parents to use this new code.

Cancelling a Joinos Code

Sometimes it is necessary to cancel a Joinos code and to do this go to Administration > Manage Connections

Find the name you wish to cancel the connection on and when open click Remove under the Disconnect Column

2 - Creating Joinos Offers

There are two ways of creating Joinos offers at the moment: version 1 (available to all) and version 2 (available to customers taking part in beta testing). Read the section for the version you are using, or use the help which is available from the screen itself by clicking on the ? and selecting View Help.

Create Offer version 1

Method: In Fund Manager Go to > Offers > Create Offer

Upload any pdf letters/documents you would like to attach to the Offer first in Offers > Upload Document

  • Select Account > Choose Account that the Offer is to be Receipted against in Fund Manager
  • VAT code > Choose if applicable
  • Select Settlement Account > Choose your Settlement Account
  • Offer Details > Give your Offer a short Title
  • Give your Offer a Description, i.e. Total cost of visit is £300. This can be paid altogether or by instalments of £50 – let your parents/carers know exactly what you want them to do, how you want them to pay and when
  • Consent required > Do you need online Consent? If this box is ticked parents/carers cannot pay unless they agree to giving online Consent
  • Amount > If the Offer is a set Amount you can specify that here
  • Unspecified > If the Offer is open to multiple payments or the parent/carer decides how much to pay – Tick the Unspecified box (if you would like your parents/carers to see what they have paid so far you will need to ‘check’ the ‘Share Balances’ tick box in Administration > Manage Accounts > Edit Account, more info here.
  • Minimum > Do you want to make sure your parents/carers can’t pay below a certain amount? If so enter this amount here
  • Offer Dates > Choose the Start Date from when you want your parents/carers to see the Offer
  • End Date > Choose the Date that you want the Offer to disappear from your parents/carers Joinos portal
  • Recurring > Do you want your parents/carers to pay again and again, if so tick the Recurring box
    • Offer Quantities
    • Min = 1 Max = 1 Means usually one pupil one time/trip/visit/occasion

    • Min = 1 Max = 2 + Means usually one or more item/ticket/place/tie can be purchased in one Transaction

    • Items Available Means usually how many seats/tickets/coach spaces are available, once sold no one else can buy, the Offer is ‘Sold Out’
    • Unlimited Means you will never exhaust supply
  • Attachments > Attach any documents you have already uploaded that you would like your parents/carers to read in conjunction with this offer
  • Select Names > Filter and tick the boxes of the Names you would like the parents/carers to pay online

You can then either:

Post Offer – parents/carers will see the Offer either immediately or after the Start Date. An email is sent to them at the end of the working day when the offer is posted, alerting them of any offers posted for their attention.

If you want to close an offer early, or cancel it, change the End Date to the current or previous day.

Abandon – To start again

Save without Posting – To Save the Offer and then return to it to Amend it at a later date

 Create Offer version 2

Method: In Fund Manager Go to > Offers > Create Offer (v2 Beta)

  • Select the Account the Offer is associated with.
  • Select a VAT Code (the default is zero).
  • Choose a Ledger, if applicable.
  • Choose the Fund Manager Settlement Account which transactions will be recorded against.
  • Enter a Title and Description for the offer. You can also choose an icon to be displayed to users within their Joinos portal.
  • Select Gift Aid if you would like to claim Gift Aid on the offer.
  • Select Consent Required if you would like your parent/carers to give online consent.
  • Select Required if you require the parent or carer to make the payment.
  • Select the Start and End Date for the offer and whether the offer can only be purchased once or can be purchased multiple times or in instalments.
  • Enter the Offer Costs, including the Total number of items you have and the Cost of the item.
  • Attach any files you would like to make available for parents to view on the offer screen. You can attach new files or files you have uploaded previously.
  • Select the Names you would like the offer to be sent to. You can use the Add Group button to add names to an offer in bulk by the Group they’re attached to.
  • When you are ready to post your offer to parents/carers, select Post this offer and click Save.

When creating offers and deciding on the cost of the trip you will need to factor in whether the school absorbs the e-payment charges or whether you include it in the cost of the offer. This would be a school audit decision.

There are different types of offers that can be created and there are examples of each given at section 3. Here is a brief description of what the options are that can be selected.

4 - Transfer Joinos payments to Bank Account ready to Reconcile

Within Fund Manager, you are required to have a Joinos Settlement Account that all of the money is imported into and this should have been set up for you before or during Training.

In Fund Manager, your monies are put into your Joinos Settlement Account, you must transfer the GROSS to your Current Account (or desired bank account) - This will show as a Debit from the Joinos account and a Credit against your Current Account.

Requirements:

  • Transfer From: Joinos Settlement Account*
  • Transfer To: Your desired bank account to reconcile
  • Amount
  • Date

Once complete, you can now select ‘ Transfer’ - The amount will now show in against the bank account selected, within the reconciliation screen.

5 - Recording Joinos Charges

After the transfer(s) are complete, you must now record any ‘bank charges’ back into the Current Account. To do this, you must have a user account setup labeled (eg Bank Charges). You should also have an organisation name to transact against (eg Joinos Bank Charges).

You must record a Payment (Direct Debit) against the Current Bank account of the amount of the Charges. You will already have transferred the GROSS amount from the Settlement Bank account which includes the e-payments and also includes your charge(s).

You MUST record these back in against your Current Account as a Direct Debit and NOT against the Settlement Account.

You will now be ready to record the transaction, to do this, go to Transactions > Record a Payment.

In this screen you will now select your user account (Bank Charges) and of course, your new name. Fill out the relevant information and the amount of your charge then select your Type as Direct Debit - Here you must ensure you bank account (in the centre of your payments screen) is your Current Account. - Finally, select Save/Save & Print.

You will now have your original gross transfer and the direct debit refund/charge against your current account ready to reconcile.

6 - Reconciling Joinos (First Data) Statements

To reconcile, confirm that the payments have been made and then any charges are entered manually. When you receive your Bank Statement which has Joinos payments in you can then move the gross amount from the Settlement Account using transfer, if you have not already done so - section 4 above.

You can enter the card fees/charges as you would any invoice in Transactions > Record a Payment and use the Direct Debit Type option see section 5 above.

  1. In your Fund Manager, your monies are put into your Joinos Settlement Account, you must transfer the GROSS to your Current Account (or desired bank account) - This will show as a Debit from the Joinos account and a Credit against your Current Account.
  2. Once you have transferred your amounts you must now record the charges. You MUST record these back in against your Current Account as a Direct Debit and NOT against the Settlement Account. Before you record the transaction, you will need to create a user account called 'Joinos Charges' and a name to match. Please enter these in your Administration screen.
  3. You will now be ready to record the transaction, to do this, go to Transactions > Record a Payment - In this screen you will now select your newly created user account (Joinos Charges) and of course, your new name. Fill out the relevant information and the amount of your charge then select your Type as Direct Debit - Here you must ensure you bank account (in the centre of your payments screen) is your Current Account. - Finally, select save.

You will now have your original gross transfer and the direct debit refund/charge against your current account ready to reconcile.

7 - How does a Parent/Carer Register and add their child?

For a Parent/Carer to be able to pay online they need to have registered with Joinos where they can make payments using a credit or debit card, view receipts for transactions and check account balances. They will always be up-to-date with your school news and events via Joinos messages and there will be no-more searching for permission slips or consent forms with online documents.

Joinos codes may expire if not used promptly so please ask them not to delay in activating their account.

They can access Joinos from their computer, tablet or mobile phone wherever they happen to be.

For a Parent to Register they will need their child’s Joinos Code which is provided by the School. There is a letter that can be provided and this can be found in Appendix A.

They register by following the instructions contained in the letter.

To add a second child logon to Joinos if necessary.

Click Settings > Add New Child > enter the Joinos code > Continue

8 - Sending the Parent/Carer an Email or SMS message

You can use Fund Manager to send the Parent/Carer an email or SMS message to inform them of important information, new/amended offers, reminders etc.

When you click on Messaging > Joinos, New Message, the following screen is shown ready for you to insert a title and text message in the Body field. You can select an attachment from the list which is already uploaded. (See section above re uploading documents).

You can also send an SMS alert by ticking the box at the bottom of the page. (This will only work where the student had a phone number listed.)

Select the students you wish to send the message to then click Send.

Confirmation is then shown at the top of Fund Manager:

The message will then appear within a very short time in the Parent/Carers Joinos screen, if they are already logged in they may need to refresh (F5) the screen to see the latest messages.

The text message received will look similar to this:

You can view the Message History which is a listing of all the Joinos Messages that have been sent. To do this, click Messaging > Joinos, History.

You can view the contents of any message by clicking on the Blue Text under the Title column. This will also show if this message has been Read by the Parent/Carer

Sending an SMS Text Message

Alternatively, you can just send an SMS message to a Parent/Carers phone.

Most, but not all, home telephones are able to receive text messages, but where possible a mobile number is preferable. You can, however, specify in Fund Manager, which phone number (where there are two or more) is the primary.

Click on Administration > Manage Names, search for the pupil, click the Manage Phone Numbers tab > choose the Primary Phone

To send an SMS Text message > Messaging > SMS, Sent Text Message > type the text message (Remember that only the standard text length - 160 characters is allowed) > Select the Student Names > Send

This will arrive at the Parents designated phone from mypebble.me and cannot be replied to.

There is also a History for all Messages that have been sent via Text Message

10 - Preparing a list of Students and Codes

In order to send a list of Students and their Joinos Codes to the Parents/Carers you will need to export this list from Fund Manager which can then be inserted into the letter (as shown in Appendix A).

To do this go to Administration > Create Joinos Codes > Joinos Codes > Export CSV.

This will produce a spreadsheet with all the information you need for the letter.
