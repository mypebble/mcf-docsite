The Importance of Stakeholder Relationships
Income may be received through your stakeholder relationships. In order to maximise the engagement and contributions from your stakeholders, you need to understand and profile the different types of relationships you have with them. You will have stakeholders to whom you are accountable. In other words you will be expected to contribute towards these bodies’ aims. You will have other stakeholders with whom you conduct transactions i.e. you will provide goods or services and in return they will give you cash, equipment, resources, facilities or materials. Finally you will have stakeholders who are looking for a deeper level of engagement and participation in your school than simply giving you money. These are known as developmental stakeholders who will want to be involved in the planning and delivery of your projects and activities.

The SRM module is where you:

  • Manage your stakeholder details, including whether they are eligible for Gift Aid and are interested in volunteering to help you out.

    • Recording stakeholders as registered for Gift Aid is a legal requirement for HMRC audit purposes. We recommend that you print the declaration forms, issue them, retrieve the signed forms and file them in a safe place to comply with legislation.

  • Create and view any marketing campaigns you have sent to stakeholders for your projects.

Create a stakeholder

Stakeholders are set up here in the SRM module, but you can also add a stakeholder through the Donations module by clicking on + Add Name when you record a donation, or + Add New when you view donations. If you have a lot of stakeholders you want to add as a batch, you can import stakeholders from a csv file, provided it is set up correctly. For more information, see Import Data.

  1. Open SRM.
  2. Click + Add New.
  3. Select the stakeholder profile from the What profile type is this contact? drop-down list.

    4. The stakeholder can be:

    • Pupil
    • Family
    • Alumni
    • Staff
    • Person
    • Business
    • Community Organisation
    • Charity
    • Person
    • Organisation
    • User

    The stakeholder profile type gives an indication of whether they are eligible for Gift Aid or to be a volunteer for your school. For example, a pupil will not be eligible for Gift Aid, as Gift Aid donors are UK taxpayers. However you may link to the Pupil for your records but enter the parent’s name as the Gift Aid donor.

    An alumni or person profile type may or may not be eligible for Gift Aid. This depends on whether or not they are retired and if they are, whether they still pay tax. For example, if a retired person has savings and pays Income or Capital Gains Tax of an equal or higher amount than that being claimed (25p in the pound), then they are eligible for Gift Aid.

    If the profile type is Staff, they will probably not volunteer to help in the school as they are already a paid employee of the school.

  4. Enter the stakeholder name and address details.

    • For Pupil, enter First Name, Last Name, Address and Postcode.
    • For Family, Alumni, Staff, Person and User, enter Title, First Name, Last Name, Address and Postcode.
    • For Business, Community Organisation, Charity and Organisation, enter Name, Address and Postcode.
  5. Click Add Contact Details.

    6. Enter the stakeholder’s Telephone Number, Mobile Number, Email Address and select the Contact Preferences which may be Letter, Email, Telephone or Mobile.

  6. If the stakeholder is a gift aid donor or volunteer, click Add Gift Aid/Volunteer Status.
    • If the stakeholder is a Gift Aid donor, select the check box This contact is eligible for Gift Aid.
      • Enter the Gift Aid donor’s Title, Donor First Name and Donor Last Name.

      You must enter this because it is a legal requirement to retain the information on who is responsible for the gift aid. If you don’t record the donor’s name here then donations aren’t recorded as donations with gift aid.

    • If the stakeholder is willing to volunteer at the school or at school events, select the Volunteer Status check box.
  7. If the stakeholder has a Twitter account which you want to record, click Add Twitter account.
    • Add the stakeholder’s Twitter username in the Twitter box.
  8. Click Create.

Setting up a Gift Aid donor

Gift Aid donors are UK taxpayers. If the donor is not a UK taxpayer, they are not eligible to give Gift Aid. This means that if your stakeholder is a pupil or a charity they are not eligible to give Gift Aid.

  1. Add a stakeholder as described in Create a stakeholder.
  2. Ensure that you click Add Gift Aid/Volunteer Status, select the check box This contact is eligible for Gift Aid and enter the donor’s Title, Donor First Name and Donor Last Name.
  3. Click Create.

You should not submit a claim to collect Gift Aid on a donation until you have received the completed declaration form back from the donor.

Download the Gift Aid declaration form

  1. Open SRM.
  2. Select and display the stakeholder details.
  3. Click Template to download the Gift Aid declaration form.

Send it to the stakeholder in the way they prefer to be contacted - Letter, Email, or Text to Mobile. If they have not stated a preference, send it using the method most likely to give a quick return. If they haven’t got an email address or a mobile phone number you will have to send it in the post.

Once you receive the signed form, ensure that you file it in a safe place as this is required for HMRC auditing purposes.

Amend a stakeholder

You can amend any of the details for a stakeholder, for example, if they change address or become a volunteer or cease being eligible for Gift Aid.

  1. Open SRM.
  2. Select the stakeholder name from the list of stakeholders.

    3. Filter the list of names displayed by entering the name or characters from the stakeholder name into the Search box.

  3. Click the stakeholder Name to display the stakeholder details.
  4. Click Edit to edit the details.
  5. Make any changes you require.
  6. Click Save.

Marking stakeholders as in or out of use

By default when you create a stakeholder, the stakeholder is in use. You can mark a stakeholder as out of use or back in use. This means that you can create and hold a full list of stakeholders even if people are occasionally not available to help you.

Mark stakeholders as out of use

  1. Open SRM.

  2. Select the stakeholder name from the list of stakeholders.

    3. Filter the list of names displayed by entering the name or characters from the stakeholder name into the Search box.

    You can select multiple stakeholders this way.

  3. Select Tools > Mark Out of Use.

Toggle the display of stakeholders to show those that are out of use

  1. Open SRM.
  2. Select the checkbox, Show Out of Use.

    3. The list of stakeholders is displayed, including the stakeholders who have been marked as out of use. Out of use stakeholders are shown in the list in grey and have a tick in the left hand column.

Mark stakeholders as back in use

  1. Open SRM.
  2. Select the checkbox, Show Out of Use.

    3. The list of stakeholders is displayed, including the stakeholders who have been marked as out of use. Out of use stakeholders are shown in the list in grey.

  3. Select the names of the stakeholders you want to mark as back in use.
  4. Select Tools > Mark Back In Use.

Import Data

If you have a lot of stakeholders to set up you can do this in one go by importing the stakeholder information from a CSV (comma separated values) file provided you set it up correctly.

You can create a Microsoft Excel spreadsheet with the extension .csv to hold the information you want to import. The first line in the file must be the header which specifies the fields you intend to import. Then each stakeholder information you want to import must be entered on a new line.

If you want to create a file of your own, you can do this by downloading the template from within the SRM module.

Records are assigned unique identifier numbers (IDs) in the import file. All records with unique IDs are appended to the existing list of stakeholders within SRM. If the ID in the import file is not unique and matches one already in SRM, then the existing record in SRM is overwritten. You can see the unique ID of existing records by exporting the stakeholder list.

Creating your own import file

  1. Open SRM > Tools > Import Data.
  2. Click Download Template.

    3. Stakeholder-Template.xslx is downloaded to your PC.

  3. Open Stakeholder-Template.xslx and save it to your PC with a name of your choosing and the Save as type CSV (comma delimited).
  4. Enter the details of the stakeholders you want to import.

    5. Remember the information is entered for one stakeholder on one line, then the information for the next stakeholder on a new line and so on, until you have entered information for all of the stakeholders.

    e.g.

    Header

    Stakeholder 1 information

    ….

    Stakeholder N information

    All entries in the spreadsheet should be in text format and IDs must be prefixed by an apostrophe (‘).

Importing data

  1. Open SRM > Tools > Import Data.
  2. Click Choose a file … and browse to select the .csv file you want to import.
  3. Select the file and click Open.
  4. Click Upload File.

    5. You will see a message saying the file has uploaded successfully.

    If you don’t see this message but instead see one which implies errors in the import file, you must go back and amend the import file to correct the errors.

    A list of stakeholders to be imported is then displayed.

    • To remove records from the import, either click Remove alongside an individual record or select a number of records by selecting the check boxes to the left of the records and then clicking Remove Selected.
    • To abandon the import, click Abandon and confirm at the prompt that you want to abandon the import.
  5. To import the records, click Import Stakeholders.

Export Data

If you have your stakeholder data in another system and you want to export the data from Arro to include in another system, you can export the data from Arro into a spreadsheet which you can save as a csv file which you can then import into another system.

  • Open SRM > Tools > Export Data.

    • The Stakeholders information is downloaded to your PC as a .xls file.

Campaigns

Campaigns help you to maximise your engagement with your stakeholders. You can run different campaigns to help you recruit volunteers, to generate excitement about your project or to promote your project to receive donations. The number of emails sent during a campaign depends on the campaign you select. Our continuous research evaluates the best times to send emails to stakeholders within the different campaigns. For example, the campaign Tell Your Story will send five emails over five weeks to your stakeholders. This campaign is included as an example below.

Run a campaign

    1. Open SRM > Campaigns.
      • To manage a project, click Manage Projects.
      • To manage your contacts, click Manage Names.
    2. Click Get Started.
    3. Select a project from the list of available projects and click Next.
    4. Choose the campaign you want to run.

Example campaigns

Tell Your Story

Run this campaign to share information about your project.

Five messages are sent about your project, over five weeks:

  1. An introduction to your project.
  2. An explanation of why your school needs your project,
  3. A description of the impact your project will have.
  4. A summary of the key benefits of your project.
  5. A final prompt to get involved with the project.

In the Summary screen, a timeline of when the messages are to be sent, is shown.

You can preview the messages that will be sent by clicking into the message title.

 Ask for Help

Run this campaign to ask your local community to help your school grow. Five email messages are sent about your project, over five weeks, in this campaign too.

View a campaign

  1. Open SRM > Campaigns.
  2. Click on a Campaign which you have run to view the details of the campaign, for example, when the email was sent, the number of emails which were opened, and so on.
