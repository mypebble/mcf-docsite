The Importance of Sponsors and Sponsorship
Sponsors can be a mainstay of project support. Partnering with businesses is a mutually beneficial endeavour – your school receives support for your projects (monetary or otherwise) and your business partner will reap rewards as well, such as fostering positive relations with their future workforce, promotional opportunities to members of your school community and fulfilling their corporate social responsibilities.

The Sponsors module is where you can manage your relationships with your sponsors and prospective major donors. The layout of the module will ensure you are always up-to-date and know who to call upon when you need to ask for support for your projects. You can search across your sponsors for those who support different types of project and keep in touch with your sponsors to let them know how their sponsorship is making a difference to your project.

Four things to keep in mind when working with Sponsors are:

  • Your Joinos Community page will be a source of potential sponsorship leads.
  • Businesses and business owners will be interested in sponsoring school projects as part of their corporate social responsibility plans.
  • Marketing campaigns can easily be set up and set as recurring within the  module to assist you in attracting sponsors on a regular basis.
  • Face-to-face networking is also a mainstay of obtaining sponsors. Every social interaction is an opportunity to spread the word that you are looking for sponsors.

Managing your sponsors

When you access the Sponsorship module, you get an overview of your leads in a pipeline dashboard. This lets you see at a glance how your sponsorship is building - how many new leads you have to work with, how many have been qualified, pledged and completed. The goal is to get as many completed as possible, so it is very encouraging to see lots of new leads coming into the pipeline to work with and rewarding to see them move up the pipeline too.

The dashboard shows:

New Leads

 

This column shows the number of leads which have been added through your Community page or added manually to the Sponsorship module.

If leads are from contacts and organisations that are not already in your  module, these details are automatically saved into the  module.

You can assign a project to a new lead.

Qualified Leads

 

Once leads have been qualified manually (been approved by the Arro project owner and have an amount to pledge to the project) they show up in the Qualified Leads column.

You can update the sponsored project details here.

Pledged

 

Once pledged leads have been confirmed and agreements have been signed or agreed to offline, the lead becomes a confirmed pledge and donations can be added here until the amount pledged amount is accrued.

As well as adding donations, you can also subtract them here by entering a negative value.

Completed

 

Once the donations reach the pledged amount, the detail moves to the Completed column.

You can also view archived leads by clicking the Archived Leads button. Archived leads are those which fail to qualify or deliver on pledged amounts.

Clicking the cards displays the card with more detail including the actions you need to move them along the pipeline. For example, leads move from New to Qualified when you click Qualify Lead, and leads move from Qualified to Pledged when you click Confirm Pledge, and so on.

How do I build a sponsor network?

When you first begin to use this module it will be empty and you need to build your sponsor network. Sponsors are added automatically when the sponsor enters their details on the Enquire Online form within the Sponsors section of your Joinos Community Page. You can build your network by creating a campaign within the module to contact your existing stakeholders in to invite them to sponsor your projects. For more information, see Campaigns. Within the Sponsors module, you can add sponsors manually.

Adding new leads

Leads are added automatically when the sponsor enters their details on the Sponsorship form on your Joinos Community Page. You can add sponsorship records manually into the Sponsorship module.

Getting sponsors through your community page

  • Ensure your Joinos Community page is published as the sponsorship form is available for selection on this page.

    • The sponsorship form collects the Name, Company, Telephone and Email address of the potential sponsor and automatically creates a sponsorship record in the Sponsors module, with a status of Prospect.

    When a sponsor is added automatically, you receive an email notification of the sponsor’s interest so that you can follow up with them, and the sponsor receives on-screen confirmation that their enquiry has been sent. You will find the sponsor name listed in the New Leads section on the Sponsorship dashboard.

Manually from within the Sponsors module

  • Ensure that you have all the sponsorship lead sheets collected.
  1. Open Sponsors and click +Add direct enquiry.
  2. Enter the Name, Company, Contact Number and Email.
  3. Click Add Lead.

    4. If you have enough information to qualify the lead you can do this now. For more information, see Qualifying leads.

Viewing and adding notes

  1. Click on the sponsor card.
    • The following details for the sponsor are shown at the top, left-hand side of the card: the sponsor name, company, source (whether the sponsor came through from Joinos community page or was manually entered from the  module or directly into the Sponsors module, the project the sponsor is supporting and the contact details for the sponsor.
    • The history of notes for the contact are shown at the bottom of the card, with the most recent note at the top of the list. The date and time are added to the message so you can track the history of your contact with the sponsor.
    • Status information on the sponsorship is displayed at the top, right-hand side of the card.
  2. To add a note, click in the Add New Note box and enter into the box the note you want to add. Then click Post. The note is added to the top of the list of notes.

Qualifying leads

Paperwork and agreements for pledges are handled offline. You can qualify leads when you have contacted and discussed with the sponsor the project(s) they are interested in sponsoring and the amount they are able to pledge. This stage also includes you sending them details of the projects they are interested in.

  1. Open Sponsors.
  2. Click the lead card in the New Leads column.
  3. Select Sent Details.
  4. Select Contacted.
  5. Select the project the lead is going to sponsor from the To Sponsor drop-down list.
  6. Select the amount the lead is going to pledge from the To Pledge drop-down list.
  7. Click Qualify Lead.

Confirming pledges

Paperwork and agreements for pledges are handled offline. Provided you have the agreed paperwork to support the confirmation, you can confirm pledges.

  1. Open Sponsors.
  2. Click the lead card in the Qualified Leads column.
  3. Check the To Sponsor selection.
  4. Check the To Pledge amount.
  5. If an initial donation has been given, enter the received donation amount.

    6. This donation cannot be Gift Aided.

  6. Click Confirm Pledge.

Adding donations

At the Pledged stage, you can add donations as and when they come in from the sponsor. Only when the total agreed amount is entered does the card move to the Completed stage.

  1. Open Sponsors.
  2. Click the lead card in the Pledged column.

    3. The amount that has been pledged and the amount that has been donated to date are displayed along with the percentage complete value so you can see at a glance how the sponsorship pledge is doing.

  3. Enter the received donation amount.

    4. If you need to reduce the donation amount, you can do this by entering a negative donation amount.

  4. Click Add Donation.

Archiving leads

Leads that don’t work out are archived so you can see the history. To see the actions you can perform on an archived lead, click the browse buttons in the Actions column alongside the archived lead.

Viewing archived leads

  1. Open Sponsors.
  2. Click Archived Leads.

Restoring archived leads

  1. Open Sponsors.
  2. Click Archived Leads.
  3. Select the lead you want to move out of the archived list.
  4. Click Move selection to New Leads.

Archiving a lead

  1. Open Sponsors.
  2. Click the lead card to archive.
  3. Click Archive.
