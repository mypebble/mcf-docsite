The Art of Grant Writing
There is approximately £1.5 billion of grant funding available to schools and educational institutions. Because grants are a one-off source of income, they are a great way of raising initial capital for projects.

The Grants module is where you can search for grants you can apply to for additional funding for your school or academy. Along with all the other sources of funding available to you, grants underpin the success of your projects. So once you locate a grant that is suitable for your needs, you must save it to a project.

We want to help you create successful grant applications. We have more than 200 grants available in our database and we usually add or update more than 20 grants per week.

Grant applications are completed outside the project, indeed outside Arro, using the resources in external websites which provide the grants or following their guidelines. However, when you apply for a grant, you should keep in mind which project it will support and understand how the grant will support the project aims. The three most important things to keep in mind when applying are:

  • To align the vision and goals of your application for your project with those of the grant donor.
  • To tell a story, identifying a need and showing the positive impact that will result from meeting that need with the grant.
  • To demonstrate planning to achieve the desired result and provide an explanation of how the result will be measured.

Where are grants listed?

All available grants are listed on the Grants dashboard in order of expiry.

Searching for a grant

You can drill down to find grants by subject (e.g. to find grants for languages or for physical education), by Grant Type, by Difficulty (the amount of detail required and the amount of time it will take to complete your application) or by Status (e.g. new, updated or expiring grants).

You can display a list of grants you have saved by clicking the grey Star icon .

When you click this to select it, the colour changes from grey to yellow. You can clear the selection and display all grants again, by clicking the icon again. It will change from yellow to grey and all the grants will be displayed again.

Or, if you know the name of the grant, you can use the Search box to enter the grant title.

You can also use the Search box to look for grants that support specific things. For example, if you need to find a grant that relates to bereavement, type bereavement in the Search box and click Search.

What types of grant are there?

There are seven different types of grant available.

Type of Grant Icon Description

Award

An award provides you with support in the form of money (e.g. Educational Grants Scheme) and/or other support (e.g. Greggs Foundation Breakfast Club) in achieving your goals.

Discount

A discount enables you to buy goods and services cheaper, or obtain cash back for the school when the discount is applied, for example, Football Foundation: Respect.

Competition

A competition is a way of providing resources to your school for the best entrant in a particular endeavour (e.g. BAFTA Young Game Designers competition).

Fundraising

There are schemes you can take part in which assist you in fundraising (e.g. Images).

Cash

Cash grants are available in particular areas of interest (e.g. Science and Plant Awards for Science teachers and technicians).

Support

There are government funded or various organisational schemes for promoting subjects (e.g. Further Maths) and enhancing the employability of young people (e.g. British Army Careers).

Freebie

Various organisations provide free resources which you can use to supplement teaching in school (e.g. Primary Science Teaching Trust).

Is a grant suitable for you?

View grant details

  • Click the Grant Title.

    • A brief outline of the award is displayed and you can see at a glance what you will get, how difficult it is to apply and whether there is a deadline or not.

    • To find out if you are eligible for the grant, read the full grant description by clicking Read More. You will also find out what you need to do next to apply for the grant. This often includes applying directly through another website. In this case, a link is supplied to the website where you will find detailed instructions on how to apply for the grant.
    • To save the grant, click Add to Starred.
    • To add the grant to your project, click Add to Project.
    • To close the grant description, click Close.

Save grants

You can save grants to access quickly by marking them as starred. You can:

  • Click the star to the left of the grant name in the list of grants . The star will turn yellow to show that you have selected it.
  • Click Add to Starred within the grant description. The star to the left of the grant name in the list of grants will turn yellow to show that you have selected it.

View saved grants

  • You can quickly view your saved grants by clicking the grey star icon at the top of the screen.
  • To access all grants again, click the yellow star icon at the top of the screen.

Apply for a grant

Applying for the grant is done outside Arro, by following the funder’s guidelines on their own website. You can access the link to the funder’s website and instructions for how to apply for a grant from within Grants or Projects, if you have attached the grant to a project.

Is a grant suitable for you?

If you are applying for a grant for one project we recommend that you access this information from within the Projects module as this sets the context for the grant application. However, if you are applying for a grant for more than one project, then you can access the information through the Grants module, but bear in mind that the application must take account of the aims of all projects the grant is for.

From within Grants

  1. Open Grants.
  2. Locate the grant you want to apply for and click the Grant Title to display an outline of information about the grant.
  3. Click Read More.
  4. Follow the information provided in the What Next section of the grant information.

From within Projects

  1. Open Projects.
  2. Select the Project.
  3. Select the Grants tab.
  4. Click the Grant Title to display an outline of information about the grant.
  5. Click Read More.
  6. Follow the information provided in the What Next section of the grant information.
