The Importance of Donors and Donations
Altruism is a means of feeling good about yourself and at the same time knowing you are helping to make a difference to a worthy cause. In addition, the UK government rewards the altruism of donation by providing relief on the tax paid on donations to charities from UK taxpayers, thereby increasing the revenue to the charity by 25%. This costs ordinary rate taxpayers nothing. Higher rate taxpayers receive tax refunds of the difference between the rate they pay and the basic rate on the donation. Gift Aid is a win-win situation.

The Donations module is where you record donations and can record and report on Gift Aid qualifying transactions. Gift Aid is a way of achieving a 25% uplift on your donations.

To benefit from the Gift Aid scheme, your organisation must be a charity or have charitable status and your donor must be a UK taxpayer. For more information and to ensure that your organisation is eligible to receive gift aid, see the HMRC requirements for claiming gift aid.

You can set up a donations button on your Joinos Web Page so that people can donate directly to your projects.

Set up a gift aid donor

Gift Aid donors are UK taxpayers. You can set up gift aid donors, using the [%=ArroModules.SRM%] (Stakeholder Relationship Management module or you can add a gift aid donor using the + Add Name option on the Record Donation form.

Access the Create Stakeholder form

Either

  • Select SRM and click + Add New.

or

  • Select Donations > Record Donation and click + Add Name.

or

  • Select Donations > View Donations and click + Add New and click + Add Name in the Donor section.

Enter the stakeholder information

  1. Select the stakeholder profile from the What profile type is this contact? drop-down list.
  2. Enter the stakeholder name and address and click Add Contact Details to add phone numbers and addresses.
  3. To ensure that the stakeholder is recorded as a gift aid donor, click Add Gift Aid/Volunteer Status and select the check box This contact is eligible for Gift Aid.
  4. Enter the gift aid donor’s Title, Donor First Name and Donor Last Name.

    5. You must enter this because it is a legal requirement to retain the information on who is responsible for the gift aid. If you don’t record the donor’s name here then donations aren’t recorded as donations with gift aid.

  5. Click Create.

Download the Gift Aid declaration form

  1. Open SRM.
  2. Select and display the stakeholder details.
  3. Click Template to download the Gift Aid declaration form.
  4. Send it to the stakeholder in the way they prefer to be contacted - Letter, Email or Text to Mobile. If they have not stated a preference, send it using the method most likely to give a quick return. If they haven’t got an email address or a mobile phone number you will have to send it in the post.

    5. You should not collect Gift Aid on donations from a donor until you have received the signed and completed Gift Aid declaration form back from them and you should retain signed declaration forms as this is a legal requirement when collecting gift aid. If you receive a donation through the Joinos Web Page, however, you won't need to collect a signed declaration form as the donor selects a checkbox when they make the online donation and that is sufficient for your records.

Record a donation

  1. Open Donations > Record Donation.

  2. Enter the Amount of the donation.

  3. Select the donation Type - Cash, Cheque, Direct Credit or ePayment.

    4. ePayment donations are made by clicking the Donations button on the Joinos Web Page for your school or academy.

  4. Select the donation Date.

  5. The donation is given a default DescriptionThis is a voluntary donation. If you want to add your own description, click Add Description and enter the description into the box that appears.

  6. Locate the donor Name.

    7. You can:

    • Add a new donor by clicking + Add Name.
    • Select an existing donor by selecting the check box to the left of the donor names listed. If you know the donor name or part of the donor name, enter this in the Search by Name box and click the Find icon .

    If the donor has been set up for Gift Aid, the Gift Aid symbol for the donor will show in colour .

Amend a donation

You can amend any of the donation information if:

  • The donation has not been submitted in a claim to HMRC.
  • The period the donation has been made within has not been closed in Fund Manager.
  1. Open Donations > Amend/Remove Donations.
  2. Find the donation that you want to amend and click the amend icon in the column at the right hand side of the screen. The Amend Donation screen appears.

  3. Enter the amendments required. You can amend any of the details.
  4. Click Amend Donations.

Remove a donation

You can remove donations if:

  • The donations have not been submitted in a claim to HMRC.
  • The periods the donations have been made within have not been closed in .
  1. Open Donations > Amend/Remove Donations.
  2. Find the transaction you want to cancel and click the Remove icon in the column at the right hand side of the screen. The Remove Donation screen appears.

    3. Check the details of the donation to make sure this is the donation you want to remove because you cannot reverse the action.

  3. When you have checked this is the donation you want to remove, click Remove Donation.

View donations

You can view the donations that have been made, including any Gift Aid for each donation.

  • Open Donations > View Donations.

    • From here you can add a new donation by clicking +Add New or view the Gift Aid by positioning the mouse cursor over the Gift Aid icon. Gift Aid provides a 25% uplift on the donation amount, so if the donation is £10 then Gift Aid is £2.50, meaning the total donation is £12.50.

Claim gift aid

You can only claim gift aid for donors who have signed and completed a Gift Aid declaration form which you retain. For more information, see Set up a gift aid donor.

When you are ready to claim Gift Aid, you can download a file of all the outstanding donations to a file which you can use to submit to the HMRC following the HMRC guidelines on submissions.

You can create as many claims as you want but for your own convenience we recommend that you create a claim at the month end. Then all the figures for that month will be held in one archive file for the month.

What is the process?

Submitting a claim has two steps:

  1. Downloading outstanding donations to a CSV file.
  2. Submitting the CSV file as a claim to HMRC.

You can also view outstanding donations and view archive claims.

What do you want to do?

View outstanding donations
  1. Select Donations > Gift Aid Claims.
  2. You will see your Outstanding Donations and any Archived Claims.

  3. To preview the outstanding donations click Preview under Reports.
 Download outstanding donations to a CSV file
  1. Select Donations > Gift Aid Claims.
  2. Click Preview under Reports.
  3. Click Download CSV Preview.
  4. Choose where to save the preview file.

    5. You can then open the file and review the details before you submit a claim.

 Submit a claim to HMRC
  1. Select Donations > Gift Aid Claims.
  2. Click Create Claim.

    • If you have received a statement from HMRC telling you that you have previously claimed too much gift aid, enter the excess you have claimed here and this is offset against the current claim.
  3. Click Save & Download to download the claim to a CSV file.
  4. Submit the CSV file to the HMRC, following the HMRC guidelines on how to do this. For more information on submission, see the HMRC guide - Schedule spreadsheet to claim back tax on Gift Aid donations.

    5. There are limits on the number of lines you can include in a single spreadsheet submitted. November 2014 guidelines state the limit is 1000 lines per file, although you can submit more than one file. This number might change so check the HMRC website for updates to ensure that you comply with the latest guidance.

 View archived claims

When you have submitted a claim, it is archived so you can't amend it but you can still view the donations through the archived Gift Aid Claim reports.

  1. Select Donations > Gift Aid Claims.

  2. Click View Report under Reports alongside the archived claim you want to see the details for.

    3. This will produce an on-screen preview of the gift aid claimed within the report.

  3. To download the claim information in the spreadsheet file, click Download Gift Aid Claim.

What if ...

I claimed back too much tax before

If you receive a letter from HMRC saying you have claimed too much gift aid, the next time you submit a claim, enter the amount in the Previously over-claimed Gift Aid box on the Create Claim screen before you click Save & Download.

The amount you enter is offset against your current claim.
