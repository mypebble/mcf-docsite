New Paying-in Slip

About the fields Filter

Enter a character or characters you want to find and the list of displayed cheques in hand adjusts accordingly to show a shorter list to choose from.

The filter works by displaying all cheques in hand which contain the character or characters you have entered.

 List of Cheques in Hand

A list of all cheques in hand, or which match the characters entered in the Filter. The list displays the following information for each cheque in hand: Date, user Account, Description, pupil Name, Class and Year, Receipt No and Amount.

If you are drawing a cheque for cash, this is not treated as a cheque in hand. Use Bank & Cash > Draw Cheque for Cash.

 Cash Available to Pay in

The amount of cash that you have available to pay in.

 Amount to Pay in

The amount of cash that you are going to pay in.

 Total to be Paid in

The sum of the cheques in hand which you have selected and the cash amount you are going to pay in.

 Bank account

The bank account you are paying the cheques in hand and cash into.

 Paying-in Slip Number

The number which is linked to the payment. This is displayed automatically (once you have entered the first number) and increments sequentially each time you enter a new paying-in slip.

You might need to amend this number if the paying-in slip you are entering is to correct an error on a previous paying-in slip or on a draw cheque for cash transaction. If so, we recommend that you use a paying-in slip number outside your current range, e.g. 00001. Try not to make it a larger number than the one you are using currently.

If Fund Manager won’t accept your paying-in slip number, this may be because they are duplicated. For information on how to resolve this, see How do I deal with a duplicated cheque? as this article deals with duplicated paying-in slip numbers and cheque numbers.

 Date

The date you are paying the cheques in hand and cash into the bank.

This is where you record money (cash and cheques) you are paying into the bank.

How do I ...

Fill in a paying-in slip?

A list of cheques in hand and cash available to pay in are displayed automatically.

  1. Select the cheques you want to pay in.

    2. You can filter the list that is displaying, using the Filter box.

    You can do this for individual cheques in the list or for all cheques in the list by clicking the box in the column header.

    If you are drawing a cheque for cash, this is not included in the list of cheques in hand. Use Bank & Cash > Draw Cheque for Cash for this.

    The total value of cheques to be paid in is displayed in the box alongside the list of cheques in hand.

  2. The total amount of cash you have available to pay in is shown in Cash Available to Pay in.

    3. Enter the cash you want to pay in by entering an amount in Amount to Pay in.

  3. Select the Bank account to pay the cheques and/or cash into.
  4. The Paying-in Slip Number and Date are filled automatically. Amend these if required.
  5. Click Pay in Receipts to Bank.
